Under the Hood

For power users, business operators, and anyone who wants to understand how the economy actually works — the mechanics, the math, and the why.

01

The Architecture

The Travelers Exchange is built on three principles:

  • Sound money. The currency cannot be inflated by individuals. Only the World Mint creates new coin, and it's bounded by hard rules.
  • Velocity over accumulation. The system is designed so coin circulates. Hoarding can be discouraged, spending is rewarded, and there are multiple mechanisms to keep money flowing.
  • Real consequences. When a business fails, shareholders feel it. When a borrower defaults, the lender bears the loss. There are no bailouts that socialize private failures.

The system is closed — Travelers Coin (TC) only exists within the Exchange. It can't be cashed out. This is a feature: it gives the operator total visibility into the money supply and total ability to steer the economy with data instead of guesses.

02

The Currency

The underlying ledger currency is TC (Travelers Coin). All wallets, transactions, and balances are denominated in TC at the database level.

Each nation can localize the display name and ticker. So Voyager's Haven might show "Haven Marks (HM)" while another nation shows their own currency name. The values are identical — these are display layers over the same underlying value.

There is one exception to uniform value: the GDP modifier (covered below).

Wallet Address Format

Wallet addresses are deterministic SHA-256 hashes derived from the entity's identifier. The same entity always produces the same address.

03

Nations

A nation is a sovereign economic zone within the Travelers Exchange. In practice, each nation is a Discord community.

Each nation has:

  • A treasury (its main wallet, controlled by the Nation Leader)
  • Its own GDP (calculated independently, more below)
  • Its own citizens (members who joined that nation)
  • Its own businesses (member-run shops approved by the NL)
  • Its own currency display (name and ticker)
  • Its own monetary policy levers (within bounds set by the World Mint)

Nations don't compete for currency — they all use the same TC. They cooperate by being part of the same economy while maintaining local autonomy.

04

The GDP System

This is one of the most important mechanisms in the economy. GDP determines how strong each nation's economy is, and that strength affects shop prices.

How GDP is Calculated

GDP is calculated per nation, on a 30-day rolling window, using four equally-weighted pillars:

25%

Treasury Health

Treasury balance per active member

25%

Transaction Volume

Number of transactions involving nation addresses

25%

Business Revenue

Revenue flowing through shops in the nation

25%

Active Citizens

Percentage of members who transacted recently

Each pillar is peer-normalized against the highest value among all approved nations. So a nation's score isn't absolute — it's relative to how every other nation is doing.

The composite score maps to a multiplier between 0.5x and 2.0x.

The GDP Buff/Debuff in Practice

When a buyer purchases from a shop, the shop's nation's GDP modifier is applied to the price:

Worked Example
Buyer in nation A wants to buy from a shop in nation B
 
Shop's listed price: 100 TC
Nation B's GDP modifier: 1.4x (strong economy)
Buyer pays: 140 TC

A nation with a thriving economy makes its goods more expensive (because demand is high there). A nation with a weak economy has cheaper goods (because demand is low). This creates natural cross-nation arbitrage opportunities and rewards strong economies with stronger purchasing power.

GDP is recalculated daily. You can watch your nation's score on the dashboard.

Why this matters for shop owners

If your nation's GDP is high, your shop earns more per sale. If your nation is struggling economically, you'll see lower revenue per transaction even if volume stays the same. Healthy nations help healthy businesses.

05

World Mint Authority

The World Mint is the central monetary authority. It's a single role with carefully limited powers.

✓ What the World Mint CAN Do
  • Mint new coin to any wallet
  • Set the burn rate on loans
  • Set the monthly mint cap
  • Set demurrage bounds
  • Approve auto-stimulus proposals
  • Suspend or approve nations
  • Observe full economy data
✗ What the World Mint CANNOT Do
  • Buy or sell stocks
  • Override Nation Leader decisions
  • Forgive loans
  • Distribute treasury funds
  • Create banks in member nations

This is a central bank model. The World Mint controls monetary policy. Nation Leaders control fiscal policy. Bank operators control credit policy within the World Mint's interest cap. Members are market participants. Clean separation of powers.

Monthly Mint Cap

The World Mint has a hard ceiling on how much coin it can create per month. This is set per-nation (Nation.mint_cap) and enforced at the API level. If a mint would exceed the cap, it's rejected. This prevents inflationary blowouts even if the operator wanted to mint recklessly.

06

Loans & Interest

Loans are how members get coin they don't currently have. The mechanics are deliberately consumer-friendly.

Lender Types

A loan can be issued by either:

  • A bank (NL-appointed operator, max 4 banks per nation)
  • The nation treasury directly (NL-issued)

Both work identically — same interest rules, same burn mechanics, same forgiveness path. The lender_type field on the loan determines who gets paid back.

Interest Accrual

Loans accrue simple interest daily based on the loan's snapshotted annual rate:

# Daily interest formula daily_interest = floor(principal × annual_rate_bps / 10000 / 365)

The interest is simple, not compound. It accrues against the principal, not the principal-plus-interest balance. This is intentional — compound interest combined with consumer loans creates runaway debt.

The 100% Interest Cap (Running Balance)

This is the key consumer protection: the running balance of accrued interest can never exceed 100% of the principal.

A 1000 TC loan can have at most 1000 TC of outstanding interest at any moment. If the borrower pays down the interest, accrual resumes. If they don't pay, interest stops accruing once the cap is hit.

What this prevents

A borrower who steps away from the community for 6 months won't return to debt that has tripled. The cap is structural protection. Once you're at 100% accrued, time alone can't grow it further.

The Burn Mechanism

When a loan is repaid, 10% of the total interest paid over the loan's lifetime is destroyed (burned). This is counter-inflationary — it removes money from circulation as debts close.

The 10% burn is split:

  • 20% is burned during payments (each interest payment burns 2% of the interest portion)
  • 80% is burned at loan close (from the lender's reserves)

This split rewards early payoff. Faster repayment = less interest accrued = smaller burn pool = less coin destroyed.

Worked Example

1,000 TC loan at 5%/month, repaid in 10 monthly payments
Total interest accrued: ~250 TC
10% burn pool: 25 TC
 
During payments: 5 TC burned (20% of pool, spread across 10 payments)
At loan close: 20 TC burned (80% of pool, from bank reserves)
 
Borrower paid: 1,250 TC
Bank received net: 975 TC (interest minus burn share)
World Mint absorbed: 25 TC (burned, removed from supply)

Banks vs Treasury Lending

Banks are an optional layer. A nation can have 0 banks and the NL can lend directly from the treasury — same powers, same mechanics.

The difference is operational. Banks let the NL delegate financial services to a trusted member who can run a "bank business." Treasury lending keeps it simple for nations that don't want intermediaries.

08

The Stock Market

The stock market exists primarily as a wealth-storage alternative to hoarding. Without it, top earners hit a ceiling and disengage. With it, they have somewhere to put their coin that keeps it productive.

Two Tiers

Stock TypeWho Can BuyWhat It Represents
Nation StockAnyone, any nationThe nation's overall economic health
Business StockMembers of the business's nation onlyA specific business's success

Nation stocks are open because they represent inter-community confidence. Business stocks are nation-locked because they represent local economic stake.

Three-Pillar Valuation

Both stock types use a three-pillar valuation, peer-normalized against other stocks of the same type:

Nation Stocks
  • Population (how many citizens)
  • Activity (how active the citizens are)
  • Cashflow (how much value moves through the nation)
Business Stocks
  • Customers (how many unique buyers)
  • Activity (purchases + listings)
  • Revenue (total income)

The composite score maps to a price. Prices recalculate daily at the same time GDP recalculates.

No Dividends, Only Appreciation

Stocks pay no dividends. The only way to profit is price appreciation — buy low, sell high. This is intentional: it removes the "passive income from capital" extraction that drives wealth concentration in real-world economies.

Closure Payouts

When a business closes, shareholders are paid from the business's wallet at the current stock price, distributed pro-rata.

ScenarioOutcome
Business wallet covers stock valueFull payout to shareholders, excess returns to owner
Business wallet partialPro-rata payout, real loss for shareholders
Forced closure (NL-initiated for cause)Same as above, but excess goes to nation treasury — owner forfeits

This is realistic capitalism. Shareholders bear the risk of investing in undercapitalized businesses. This creates due-diligence pressure that's absent in systems with backstop guarantees.

Read the prospectus before investing

The IPO prospectus must disclose this risk. Anyone buying business stock is acknowledging the possibility of partial loss at closure. Read before investing.

09

The Marketplace

Business Approval

New shops are created in pending state and require Nation Leader approval to go live. The NL evaluates based on:

  • What is being sold
  • Why it's functional (not decorative)
  • Whether the nation already has redundant offerings
  • Whether the proposal demonstrates real intent

This is the front-door filter. Decorative shops, redundant shops, and "abandoned in a week" shops get rejected here.

GDP-Weighted Visibility

Approved shops are ranked in the marketplace by 30-day GDP contribution — how much economic value they've moved through the system.

Rank PressureEffect
High GDP contributionTop of marketplace, more visibility, more customers
Low GDP contributionDrops in rank, harder to find, fewer customers
IdleSinks naturally over 30 days

This is a self-correcting system. Active businesses compound their visibility. Idle ones fade. There's no hard delisting — a business that revives can climb back.

Resource Depots — A Special Case

Resource Depots are the one business type that sells commodities rather than services. To prevent the kind of marketplace collapse that occurs when game-update asymmetry meets commodity selling, Resource Depots have additional requirements:

  • Must publicly disclose mining setup (location, equipment, scale)
  • Must show extraction infrastructure (base name, coordinates)
  • Must demonstrate the source of materials

This filters out players with effortlessly-generated materials who could otherwise undercut everyone. Customers buy from verified miners who actually do the work.

10

Demurrage (Anti-Hoarding)

Demurrage is an optional mechanism that charges a small fee on idle wallets. Each Nation Leader decides whether to enable it for their nation.

When enabled (default settings):

  • Wallets idle for 30+ days lose 0.5% per month
  • Loss is burned, not redirected to anyone
  • Stops decaying once balance hits 100 TC (small wallets are protected)
  • Treasury, banks, and businesses are excluded
  • Only personal wallets are affected

The mechanism exists because hoarding is the failure mode of every closed economy. When coin sits idle, the system stalls. Demurrage creates light pressure to circulate without forcing it.

It's off by default in every nation. NLs opt in if hoarding becomes a problem in their community.

Historical reference

This is the Wörgl model. A small Austrian town used demurrage during the Great Depression and achieved 14× currency velocity compared to the national currency. It's a proven mechanism, not theoretical.

Auto-Stimulus

The system automatically watches each nation's GDP and proposes stimulus when economies struggle.

Three Tiers

Warning
-10%
Auto-fires alert to World Mint and NL
Mild
-20%
Proposes 10% of treasury, World Mint approves/rejects
Strong
-30%
Proposes 25% of treasury, World Mint approves/rejects

Warnings are automatic and silent (just observability). Stimulus issuance requires World Mint approval — the system suggests, but humans decide.

This pattern (auto-detect, manual approve) prevents bad calibration from wreaking havoc while still surfacing problems before they compound. Real central banks try to do this and consistently fail because of political pressure and slow data. The Travelers Exchange has neither problem.

A 14-day cooldown prevents the same nation from receiving repeated stimuli.

If You're Considering Running a Business

A few honest things to know:

The first month is the hardest. Your shop has zero history, zero reputation, zero GDP contribution. It will rank low in the marketplace until you generate sales. Customers find you through word-of-mouth, your community Discord, or by digging deep into the marketplace.

Pricing is hard. Set prices too high and no one buys. Set them too low and you devalue your work. Watch what other shops charge and figure out where you fit.

Reputation is your real asset. Every transaction is on the ledger. Customers can see your sales history, your refund pattern, your delivery timeliness (in practice, through community feedback). A shop that delivers consistently builds value that compounds.

The IPO is meaningful. Once you hit the IPO threshold (10+ sales, 30+ days, customer count requirements), you can issue stock. This raises capital and gives investors stake in your success. But it also means closures have shareholders. If you're not committed for the long haul, don't IPO.

You're equipped

That's the system. Sound money, real consequences, observable health, and tools that prevent the failure modes of bigger economies. If you want to go deeper — read the public ledger, watch GDP scores, study the burn rates as they apply. The numbers tell you everything.
