A Proposal for Nation Leaders

Why your community might want to adopt the Travelers Exchange — and what you'd actually be agreeing to.

01

What This Is

The Travelers Exchange is a community-built economic platform for No Man's Sky communities. It's already running. Voyager's Haven is the launch nation. We're inviting other established communities to join as sovereign nations within the same economy.

This document explains what you'd be adopting, what you control, what you don't, and why we think it's worth your community's time.

No catch.

We're not selling anything. There's no fee. There's no token sale. The Exchange is community infrastructure built to be adopted.

02

The Short Version

If you're short on time, here's the pitch in five points:

  • Your community gets a working economy — wallets, shops, loans, a stock market, a public ledger — without you having to build any of it
  • You stay sovereign — you control your treasury, your members, your businesses, and your community's display name and ticker for the currency
  • The currency is shared — your members can transact with members of other nations; the underlying value is uniform across nations
  • The economic model is honest — no inflation games, no hidden inflation, no debt traps; the system is designed for sustainable circulation
  • You can leave — if it doesn't work for your community, you withdraw your nation; you don't owe anyone anything

If that's enough, talk to us. The rest of this document is for leaders who want to understand the details before committing.

03

The Problem We're Solving

Most community economies fail. The pattern is consistent:

Failure ModeWhat Happens
Game-update asymmetryA game change makes player effort uneven (e.g., difficulty settings, easy-mode vs hardcore). Easy-mode players can undercut everyone, and the marketplace loses coherence.
Decorative shopsMembers create shops just because they can, abandon them in a week, marketplace becomes a graveyard.
Hoarding without pressureActivity-based currencies with no anti-hoarding mechanisms let top earners accumulate, and the economy stalls.
Centralized fragilityOne operator burns out, the whole thing dies.

We've watched these patterns play out across multiple community economies. The Travelers Exchange is built specifically to avoid each of these failure modes structurally — not through promises or willpower, but through design.

How Sovereignty Works

Your nation is yours. You don't lose authority by adopting the platform.

What You Control

AuthorityDetail
Member approvalOnly people you approve become citizens of your nation
Currency displayYou name the currency and set the ticker for your community
TreasuryYou hold and distribute your nation's funds
Business approvalMembers create shops; you approve them or don't
Bank operatorsYou appoint them — banks are optional, you don't have to use them
Loan ratesSet within the World Mint's interest cap, you decide what your banks charge
DemurrageYou decide whether to enable it for your nation
Distribution policiesHow monthly allocations get split among citizens is your call
Discord integrationThe Keeper bot operates on your terms in your Discord

What You Don't Control

AuthorityWhy
Burn rate on loansWorld Mint sets globally — keeps the supply math consistent
Mint capWorld Mint sets — prevents inflation from any nation
Demurrage boundsWorld Mint sets the ceiling and floor; you set within that
Stock market mechanicsSame math applies to all nations — fairness
Public ledgerPublic, immutable; you can't hide your nation's transactions

What You Can Do Without Asking Permission

  • Reject any business application
  • Forgive any loan
  • Suspend any shop in your nation
  • Change your distribution policies
  • Toggle demurrage on or off
  • Promote, demote, or remove members
  • Withdraw your nation entirely
05

The Money

Where the Coin Comes From

There are two ways coin enters your nation's economy:

  • World Mint allocation — The World Mint mints coin to your treasury based on your member count and activity. You distribute it to members per your policies.
  • Member earnings — Citizens earn coin from each other through transactions, business sales, stock appreciation, and loans they later pay back.

Where the Coin Goes (When It's Destroyed)

Some coin is burned (permanently destroyed) by design:

  • 10% of total interest paid on loans, split 20% during payments and 80% at close
  • Demurrage on idle wallets, if your nation enables it
  • Various tax mechanisms

Why this matters

This is counter-inflationary. The supply doesn't grow uncontrolled. The system has structural deflationary pressure that balances the World Mint's minting authority.

What's Worth Knowing

  • Currency is integer-only — no fractional coin (avoids floating-point precision issues)
  • Coin cannot leave the system — there's no cash-out, no exchange to real currency
  • Your community's coin and another community's coin are the same underlying currency with different display names

This is locked in by design

It protects your members from the failure mode that destroys other community economies.

07

Inter-Nation Trade

Your members can transact with members of other nations. They can buy from another nation's shops. They can hold stock in another nation. The currency works everywhere.

The one nuance is the GDP modifier. Each nation has its own economy strength, calculated daily. When buying from a shop in another nation, the modifier affects the price. A shop in a strong economy charges more (because demand is higher there). A shop in a weak economy charges less.

This isn't a gotcha — it's a natural consequence of economies operating at different scales. Your members will see the conversion at purchase time. Nothing is hidden.

What this means in practice

Your members can always tell whether they're getting a deal by buying from a weaker economy or paying a premium for a stronger one. It's transparent and informative.

08

The Keeper Bot (Optional)

We've built a Discord bot called Keeper that integrates with the Travelers Exchange. It does two things:

  • Tracks XP for community contributions in your Discord
  • Awards Travelers Coin to your members based on that XP

Keeper draws from your nation treasury — you set the per-XP rates within your community. If your nation runs out of coin, awards stop until you allocate more.

Keeper is invitation-only. Your community has to invite the bot to your Discord, and you control its configuration. Other nations have their own coin-award mechanisms; they don't need Keeper.

Keeper is optional

You can adopt Travelers Exchange without Keeper. Many nations might choose to award coin manually through their NL or via their own existing community tools. Keeper makes the process automatic, but it's not required.

09

How We Protect Your Community

The Travelers Exchange has structural protections that other systems lack:

Against Hoarding

  • GDP-based pricing rewards velocity — money that moves makes the system stronger for everyone
  • Optional demurrage lets you charge a small fee on idle wallets if hoarding becomes a problem
  • Stock market gives high earners somewhere to put their coin that isn't hoarding

Against Debt Traps

  • 100% interest cap — a loan can never grow beyond 2× the original principal
  • Pay-whenever interest — borrowers don't face crushing minimum payments
  • NL forgiveness — you can forgive loans for genuine hardship cases

Against Exploitation

  • Public ledger — every transaction is visible and verifiable
  • Three-pillar valuation — stocks track real performance, not hype
  • No bailouts — businesses that fail bear the consequences; this prevents moral hazard

Against Inflation

  • Monthly mint cap — the World Mint can't print coin recklessly
  • Burn mechanisms — money is destroyed as debts close
  • No fractional banking — banks can only lend what they hold

Against Catastrophic Loss

  • Auto-stimulus — automated detection of struggling nations with proposed interventions
  • Counter-cyclical injection — the World Mint can issue stimulus to nations whose economies are slowing

What Joining Looks Like

Joining as a nation is a deliberate process. Here's the path:

StepWhat Happens
1. ConversationYou and the World Mint operator discuss your community size, goals, fit, and what roles your leadership team would play
2. ApplicationYou submit a nation application — name, currency name, ticker, leadership
3. ReviewWe review for fit (no name collisions, etc.)
4. ApprovalYou're approved. Treasury wallet created, nation stock auto-issued, your nation appears in the network
5. Member onboardingYour members register, apply to your nation, you approve them
6. CalibrationFirst month is conservative — allocations adjust as we see real activity

This process takes about a week end-to-end if everyone moves quickly.

Questions You Probably Have

"What if our community doesn't like it?"

You withdraw your nation. We delist it. Your members keep their wallets but no further activity happens. There's no penalty, no fee, no contractual obligation. Adoption is permission-based, not commitment-based.

"What if the operator (World Mint) burns out or goes inactive?"

This is a real risk we're actively mitigating. The codebase is open within our community network, so transitioning operators is technically possible. We're documenting succession protocols. Honest answer: a small system depends on its operator. If you'd want this to outlive any one person, we're working on that.

"What if you change the rules later?"

The locked decisions document is canonical and shared with all NLs. Changes to monetary policy go through a public process. You see them coming, you have input, and you can withdraw your nation if a change is unacceptable.

"What about disputes between nations?"

There's no formal arbitration process yet because there haven't been formal disputes. As the network grows, this will need formalization. Right now, leaders talk. We'll codify when we need to.

"Will my community's data be safe?"

The system is hosted on a Raspberry Pi in a private homelab, accessed via a public domain. The codebase isn't public. Your members' wallet activity is on the public ledger (by design — the ledger is the trust mechanism), but personal information beyond username isn't exposed.

"What if NMS fundamentally changes?"

The Travelers Exchange is designed to survive game changes because it doesn't depend on game-internal economics. Currency is external. XP awards are tied to verifiable submissions, not in-game value. If NMS changes how Sentinels work tomorrow, the Exchange continues unaffected.

"Is this a get-rich-quick thing?"

No. The currency is sandboxed. There's no real money. Your members can't sell their TC for USD. This is a community engagement layer, not an investment vehicle. Anyone selling it as the latter is misrepresenting it.

"What's in it for you (the operator)?"

A working community economy that benefits Voyager's Haven and any nation that adopts it. The cost is real (Pi hosting, my time, ongoing maintenance). The benefit is the same as why we built Haven Control Room: better community infrastructure makes everyone's experience better. Not making money from this. Just want it to exist and work.

Who This Is Right For

Consider joining if:

  • ✓Your community is established (active members, regular activity)
  • ✓You want to give your members an economic layer they don't currently have
  • ✓You're willing to do the work of approving members and businesses
  • ✓You believe in transparent monetary policy with hard rules
  • ✓You want to be part of a network of communities, not just a standalone

Probably skip if:

  • ✗You want full control with no shared monetary policy
  • ✗You want a token sale or real-money currency
  • ✗Your community is too small or inactive for an economy to make sense
  • ✗You don't have time to be an active Nation Leader

The Honest Bottom Line

The Travelers Exchange is a working system that already serves a real community. We've fixed bugs. We've audited the code three times. We've smoke-tested 52 scenarios. The economic model is grounded in real-world economic principles (Wörgl-style anti-hoarding, central-bank-style monetary controls, blockchain-style auditability) and is honest about its tradeoffs.

It's not perfect. Nothing is. But it's better than building your own from scratch, and it's better than nothing.
