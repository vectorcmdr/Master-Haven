# For Civilization Leaders

If you're reading this, you probably run a No Man's Sky community on Discord — anywhere from a few dozen Travelers to several thousand. This document is the pitch for bringing your civilization into Haven as a recognized partner. What you'd be adopting, what you'd control, and what we'd ask of you.

## What Haven Offers Your Civilization

Haven is the closest thing the No Man's Sky community has to a shared library of explored space. Bringing your civilization in means:

- **A dedicated community page** at `/community-stats/<your-tag>` showing your members, your regions, your activity timeline, and your share of the universe — separate from any other civilization's stats.
- **Branded badges** on every submission your members make. When a Voyager from your community submits a system, Haven labels it with your color and your tag.
- **A scoped admin queue.** Your civilization's submissions go to *your* queue. Other communities don't see them unless you choose to make them public, and you don't have to triage anyone else's backlog.
- **Galactic posters.** Auto-generated weekly posters showing where your civilization has been. Nice for Discord announcement channels.
- **Optional Keeper integration.** Keeper is the Discord bot that bridges Haven and your server — slash commands for personal stats, contribution tracking, leaderboards. You can opt in or stay out.

## What You'd Control

You stay in charge of your own community. Haven's role is the platform; the civilization is yours.

- **Your tag, color, and identity.** You pick the badge color, the display name, and the abbreviation that appears across the platform.
- **Your sub-admins.** You can promote up to several of your members to *Sub-Admin* — they can review and approve submissions on your behalf without seeing other civilizations' data. Useful when you don't want to be the only person clearing a queue.
- **Your data restrictions.** If there's a class of submission you don't want public — say, base coordinates of a private build — you can restrict it to community-only visibility. The data still lives in Haven; only your members see it.
- **Your members' privacy.** Members choose whether to set a password. Read-only profiles let people contribute without ever logging in.

## What You Wouldn't Control

We're honest about the boundaries.

- **The platform itself.** Haven is built and operated by Voyager's Haven leadership. Major architectural changes — schema migrations, new endpoints, hosting moves — are our call.
- **Cross-community data.** A system submitted by your members is your civilization's contribution, but the *data* itself (glyphs, planets, biomes) is shared with every other community. We don't fragment the universe.
- **The map's visual style.** The 3D map and the public site share a single look across all civilizations. You can't reskin Haven, but your civilization's branding shows up everywhere your members' contributions appear.
- **Other civilizations' members.** Haven is a multi-tenant platform. We don't mediate disputes between communities; we just give each one its own space to work.

## What We'd Ask of You

Realistic asks. Nothing onerous.

- **A point of contact.** One person — usually you — who can answer questions when something needs a decision (a sub-admin promotion, a data restriction request, a moderation question).
- **A modest review pace.** Submissions in your queue should get reviewed within a few days. If your community is too small to keep up, we'll help — Voyager's Haven admins can be granted shadow access to your queue as a fallback.
- **Honest data.** No knowingly fake submissions, no system-name spam, no glyph forgery. The standard is "if it's worth submitting, it's worth getting right."
- **A welcoming posture.** Cross-civilization friendliness is part of the deal. Members of other communities will sometimes browse your data; we ask that you treat their contributions with the same respect you'd want for your own.

## How the Onboarding Works

Bringing a civilization in is a conversation, not a form.

1. **Reach out.** DM Ekimo (the lead) on Discord, or post in `#partnerships` on the Voyager's Haven server.
2. **Talk it through.** A short call (30 minutes is plenty) covering: how big your community is, how active your members are with NMS exploration, what your goals are.
3. **Pilot run.** We provision your civilization tag, set up the partner account, and your first sub-admin. Members start submitting under your badge from day one.
4. **Tune as you go.** First month is for adjustments — community color, queue handling, who reviews what. Nothing is locked in.
5. **Stay or step back.** If it isn't working for your community after a couple of months, we unwind cleanly. Members keep their contributions; the badge gets retired.

## What This Is Not

A few things we want to be clear about up front, because the No Man's Sky community has been burned by some of these before.

- **It's not a paid platform.** Haven costs nothing to use. We don't charge civilizations, members, or anyone else.
- **It's not a closed garden.** Data is community-shared by default. The point of Haven is that exploration *aggregates* — your members' work makes the platform better for everyone, and vice versa.
- **It's not run by a company.** Haven is built by hobbyists in our spare time. We have a roadmap and we ship steadily, but there are no SLAs and no support contracts. We're motivated by enjoying the work and serving the community, not by revenue.
- **It's not a replacement for your Discord.** Your community's Discord is still the heart of your civilization. Haven is the cartography arm — one tool in your kit, not the kit itself.

## Final Word

Haven exists because a small group of Voyagers wanted a better map. It works because civilizations chose to join the experiment and bring their members along. If your community is doing real exploration in No Man's Sky and you want a place to put that work — somewhere it'll be preserved, shown to the rest of the community, and recognized by your civilization's identity — that's what Haven is for.

Reach out. We'd like to hear what you're building.
